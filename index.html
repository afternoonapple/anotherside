Prologue

Nothing out of the Ordinary


Somewhere in the distance you hear an echoing, resonant sound you are all too familiar with. That stupid bell that rings at the end of each period.

However, in some extremely rare cases such as this one, you would consider the bell not a symbol of impending death, but a welcome end to all the death that has occurred prior. Death of your brain cells, that is. You think you’ve done enough calculus for a lifetime, and by that you mean not only are you willing to throw all this knowledge out a window for the foreseeable future, you’ll feel like kicking the bucket if you try to cram any more in.

After the teacher dismisses the class, and before you realize what is even going on, students bolt out of the room in uncanny groups of three or five. In fact, you only fully awaken from your nice afternoon nap when your “beskie”, as she calls it, bestows upon you the splendid and bountiful gift of one swift smack to the back of your head.

BESKIE: hey, friendo.

BESKIE: class is over.

FRIENDO: Huh, is that so…

BESKIE: yeah, didn’t you hear the bell?

BESKIE: remember you once said “it ingrains itself into the subconsciousness of even the most mentally uncompromised” or something like that.

BESKIE: not even a sleepy sleepy owl like you couldn’t have heard it.

FRIENDO: Again with the owl stuff?

BESKIE: sorry, couldn’t help myself…

She shrugs helplessly, at the whim of her own imagination.

BESKIE: it’s just you’re always so silent… and yet so sudden sometimes.

BESKIE: that’s what i like about you ;) hehe

OWL: Well I do appreciate the metaphor.

OWL: Also thanks for waking me up, I guess.

BESKIE: no problem buddy :3

BESKIE: you finally gonna join the class for lunch? or you’re sitting at the small round table again.

BUDDY: Eh I think I’ll keep the status quo.

BESKIE: aww man you’re being an owl again?

BESKIE: thought if there was any day of the year you’d like to make a change it would’ve been today…

BESKIE: happy birthday, by the way. don’t think i forgot. :)

BUDDY: Heh, thanks Sophie. I can always count on you to remember.

SOPHIE: hey, call me your beskie. or bestie, or deskie.

BUDDY: Alright, "beskie". Whatever floats your boat.

SOPHIE: aw thank you, zeph.

SOPHIE: see you around the cafeteria then, eh?

ZEPH: See you there.

She gets up and leaves the classroom. By this time, you are the last person left.

Slowly, you shake off the lethargy accumulated from math period like a heavy blanket of snow, and head down to the cafeteria to get a much, very much needed bite.

While walking down the stairs your mind can’t help but reflect on what kind of conversation you had with Sophie earlier. That might just have been the most sincere conversation you two have ever had since you first sat together about three months ago. You know you tend to be pessimistic at times, but genuinely you are puzzled at how she hasn't gotten tired of your personality yet. It's probably something that comes with the benefit of being "deskies", you guess.

On certain bouts of frequent self-introspection such as this one, you can't help but wonder if those who say they appreciate you are hiding something from you, as if they're only tolerating you and waiting for the right moment to break off when they think you won't notice. A very dangerous thought if you've ever thought about one. Ah, there you go again, being pessimistic.

It's okay, no one has to know. Your life, as much as you would like to believe it could be, isn't some grand dramatic story wherein you are the main character and your friends, "beskie" included, are side characters helping you in your quest to attain Maslow's topmost tier.

Although, now that you think about it… If you were part of a work of great fiction, who would you paint yourself to be? How would you introduce your character? You think having to keep up a "character" with a certain "personality" all the time would tire you out. As your "beskie" said, you prefer to be "sudden". But who would even deem you entertaining enough to watch anyway? No one except the bored, the aimless, and those with too much time to kill, you presume. That's why you’re 98% sure your existence couldn't possibly live up to a fictional title. And yet, you have a knack for trying (successfully or not) to prove yourself wrong.

Your name is Zephaniah Aisling, although Zephaniah is quite a mouthful, so on the rare occasion that someone actually uses your name in conversation they use the convenient short form Zeph.

Long story short, you’re a pretty weird guy. At least that’s the impression the rest of the class gives you. You can’t help but feel every time you want to socialize, you unwittingly get blocked by someone else. And every time you do, people look at you like you’re a made up character in a storybook. Of course, you don’t blame them. You rarely blame anyone, because you know it’s most probably your fault. Confidence just doesn’t come as easily to you as it does to some other people you know.

You reach the ground floor after what feels like a one-odd page of exposition and head towards the cafeteria, where you figure practically everybody with a social life is probably currently at. Ah, lunch period. A reprieve from the horrors of the three-hour sensory assault before it. A refreshing stream in the middle of a haunted forest. An oasis of respite in the middle of a scorching desert. Before you can cook up any more metaphors you arrive at the place where they cook up actual food, and find your rightful place at a peculiar bench for four, somehow always half-covered in sunlight and half-covered in shade. However, just like any usual day, it serves its function as a bench for one.

You put your schoolbag down and head to the stalls to get some food. On the way back you spot one Sophie Keller waving rather enthusiastically across the cafeteria. You still don’t think you’re ready to join her group of friends just yet. Firstly, a substantial number of them are from the neighbouring class and so they don’t really know who you are, which intimidates you. Secondly, and arguably more importantly, they’re mostly girls, which intimidates you further.

You want her to know that you’ve acknowledged and appreciated her gesture, and yet establish that you’re not really feeling up to it right now. So in a very Zephaniac fashion you stare back at her, raise your eyebrows quizzically and walk in the opposite direction. At least from what you see out of the corner of your eye, she returns the eyebrows, so you hope she gets what you mean.

You have a pretty decent and average lunch. It’s the same bowl of rice with the same few meats and vegetables as you had around this time last week. No harm can come from the familiar, you always say. Plus, it’s one of the less-patronized stalls in the cafeteria, so not only do you never have to queue up for your food, you’ve convinced your altruistic side that you’ve made some contribution to that kind old lady chef’s revenue.

After you finish your food and return your bowl and utensils to the collection area like a Good Boy, you hang around idly and contently for another half an hour or so. Not having to entertain a large group of friends every lunch break can be liberating, you think. Imagine being there and, like, talking to people and all that. Heh. It would be really nice, but you already know every time you tell yourself you’re going to make a change tomorrow you’ll just end up repeating what you’ve already said the previous day.

You get your bag and start walking off to the auditorium where the last (and definitely most exciting) period of the day, Drama, will be held. If all goes according to plan, which it usually does, you’ll get there about 10 minutes before most others. But, you never know. Maybe since it’s your birthday the universe will decide to surprise you or give you a gift.

Halfway there you feel a tap on your right shoulder, accompanied by a familiar, sing-song voice.

VOICE: Hey, zeph :)

ZEPH: Oh, hey Avis.

AVIS: Being early as usual, i see.

ZEPH: Hmm yeah.

ZEPH: You’re early today too though. I thought you’d still be with your friends in the cafeteria around now.

AVIS: Well, i told them i’d be ditching them a little early just for drama these few weeks

AVIS: Gotta prepare for the stage rehearsal.

ZEPH: Oh yes, you are the titular Mockingbird from the hit play The Mockingbird by Samsara Fayruz. How could I forget?

AVIS: Heheh~

She chuckles: a warm, distinctive laugh.

AVIS: Are you jealous?

ZEPH: Jealous? No way.

ZEPH: If anything, I’m horrible at acting.

AVIS: Hmm, gotta work on confidence then.

AVIS: I don’t know, acting is just kind of a mindgame

AVIS: It’s like a bunch of people decided to drop you straight into some weird fever dream and the person who plays along the best gets rewarded.

ZEPH: Yeah, it’s all about suspension of disbelief, right?

AVIS: The what?

ZEPH: You know, the suspension of disbelief.

AVIS: Ehh i don’t know what that means. I’m not that good on the vocabulary

ZEPH: Well it’s quite simple--

AVIS: Hey on second thought i don’t think i want to know. LOL

You force out a smile, but it’s a genuine one.

AVIS: Anyway, yeah. Guess in this class i’m that person who came out on top. Just hope i deliver for the festival.

ZEPH: Eh I’m sure you’ll pull it off.

AVIS: Really?

ZEPH: Well yeah, there’s clearly a reason you were selected. I guess they see something special in you.

You considered saying “we”.

AVIS: Awww. You’re being honest?

ZEPH: Honesty is one of my only redeeming qualities.

AVIS: Wow. Maybe that’s why you’re bad at acting.

ZEPH: Heh.

You two reach the auditorium, still early, but around three minutes later than your usual time. A negotiable amount.

AVIS: Alright, I gotta go get my costume and makeup done and all that.

AVIS: Catch you in the lights and sounds booth.

ZEPH: Wow, you’ve like, acknowledged that we exist.

AVIS: Do people not notice you guys?

AVIS: You, soph, 2 or 3 others?

ZEPH: I assure you, they don’t.

AVIS: Aw, maybe later in the play the mockingbird will fly up off the stage into the lights and sounds booth and give everyone in there a high five.

ZEPH: We shall see.

AVIS: Heehee~ Ok i’ve gotta go

AVIS: Wish me luck

ZEPH: Yeah don’t worry about it, you have what it takes. I mean, that’s why we selected you for lead role in the first place, right?

AVIS: Aww, thanks~

AVIS: I’ll be sure to do my best for you guys :)

ZEPH: All the best.

She slinks into the actors’ room backstage and closes the door behind her with an almost imperceptible click.

You are actually quite impressed at how you held up against her. Avis’ personality is definitely not one you’d trifle with. It feels like her vitality radiates from her in an aura, almost like she’s bringing out the confidence in you whenever you talk. Anyway, that’s about the third or fourth time you’ve talked since you two have met, so you can’t really make generalizations. You do admire her for that, though. Even when you first met her she already had something you’d never see yourself having. Almost like it’s effortless.

 

Everyone in the class has been so busily preparing for the Drama Festival that takes place near the end of the first semester. You feel a little pity for the aesthetic departments: when in comparison to whatever you’re doing, it feels like they’re doing way more work. Regardless, it’s not your business to judge.

After everyone arrives about 5 minutes after the formal start of the period (as usual), your class' drama teacher, Mr Zac, gives a pretty long briefing on how the opening scene should feel like. Theoretically, everybody in the class already knows this, but this is the first time you guys have practiced, so you guess a reminder would do everyone some good. At some point the actors come out and you spot Avis and the other "characters" all in full costume. You must say, they look completely different under the spotlight on the stage, it's almost stunning. Looks like the costume people really pulled through on this one.

MR ZAC: Alright children, rehearsal is the most important thing today. After this, we're done. LNS guys, get to the booth. Actors prepare yourselves. Rehearsal will begin in 5 minutes. Everything good? Ok, get going!

All the students scramble to their places. You do a sound check in the booth, and everything goes fine. Sophie runs a lights check too, and she gives everyone a thumbs up to let the crew know everything is fine too, although it seems more like she is on the verge of giving you a hug.

The rehearsal begins.

Curtains open and everyone goes silent. A dim yellow light casts its rays onto the floor of center stage, growing in intensity until it floods the whole set. Meanwhile, your audio crew plays a track of birds humming and chirping in the distance. The result is a serene image of a morning forest, basking in its own glory, teeming with flora and fauna.

You lean forward in your chair, attentive to the impending cue…always 17 seconds in.

14…15…16...17. You switch to another track.

Let the show begin.

Anotherside

Chapter I

Fright or Flight


Lights flicker red at the push of a button. The music jumps into a flurry of entangled notes, softly at first but with the promise of crescendo soon to come. Entering from the left starts a mockingbird, endowed with a single vibrant blue feather. She clumsily flies through the foliage to escape the hunter trying to capture her. While she evades the hunter’s efforts, with her stamina fast depleting-- while she thinks it is safe, she is finally caught.

MOCKINGBIRD: What are you doing, child? Please, please, release me!

CHILD: Why should I? You are a fine specimen of a mockingbird.

MOCKINGBIRD: I have been flying, flying away from my home, for I am no longer welcome there. Now there is a horrible, horrible hunter coming to strip me of my last blue feather.

CHILD: Do not be afraid, little mockingbird. I will keep you safe.

MOCKINGBIRD: You will?

CHILD: I promise.

HUNTER: WHO BE THERE?

The hunter appears from behind a shrub, his voice looming over the pair of younglings as equally as his stature.

HUNTER: Boy, have you seen a tiny little mockingbird around here?

CHILD: No, I have not.

HUNTER: No? It was just flying around these parts. It is a vile creature, unlike the rest of its kind. I am only finding it for its one blue feather.

CHILD: Oh, well then I have seen the bird.

HUNTER: You have?

CHILD: It was flying through the trees,  in the direction you came from. I did not know it was a mockingbird.

HUNTER: Excellent. It cannot go much further than the clearing. Thank you for your guidance.

CHILD: You’re welcome, sir.

The hunter continues his trek in search of the vile creature. With that, the child carries the beautiful mockingbird in his arms, and returns home.

MR. ZAC: Hey, that was pretty good! Good job Avis and Jackson. Michael, you need to be more unfeeling. Not exactly bright and angry.

MICHAEL: Sure thing, sir!

MR. ZAC: Other than that, nice work.

MR. ZAC: Ok, that's all for today people. Let's pack up and leave this place. The sun’s going to set soon, and I wanna eat my dinner on time. Class dismissed!

STUDENTS: Thank you Mr Zac!

The students keep the props, close the curtains and switch off the lights. As the three actors take off their masks and start chattering about the events of the day, a shy boy walking down the steps can’t help but look at the trio and wonder what it would be like if he was among them, if only for a minute.

Another nice acting job by the three characters, it seems. While you’ve never been a big fan of theatre, you appreciate all the effort it takes to keep up those appearances. You’re terrible at pretending, you think. You’d love to be on stage, commanding the attention of a whole audience, but if the past 17 years of your life have told you anything, it’s that it’s not a good idea to have so many eyes on you at any one time. That’s why when the class decided to do The Mockingbird for the upcoming Drama Festival your school holds every year, you immediately defaulted to the Lights and Sounds section. Ironically, it’s the darkest and quietest section of the play when it’s actually running. Nonetheless you absolutely love your time there, because drama is one of the only periods where you don’t feel like someone is watching you, waiting for you to do something stupid.

Anyway, enough about you. Now’s not the time to metaphorically beat yourself up, you already did that a few nights ago. Also, today’s your birthday for goodness sake! You’re allowed to celebrate what little character strengths you have, if only for a single day.

You walk out the school gate with a little more spring in your step than usual, catching your neighbour and close friend near the bus stop.

ZEPH: Hey, Alex!

ALEX: oh heeey. what’s up, bro?

ZEPH: Nothing much. Just got off the last period which was drama.

ALEX: man, I love drama. you know, the Drama Fest is coming up pretty soon.

ALEX: what play is your class doin’?

ZEPH: Oh, we’re doing The Mockingbird by Samsara Fayruz.

ALEX: haven’t heard of it. my class really wanted to do 1984.

ZEPH: Wasn’t that a novel?

ALEX: yeah, the original was written by some guy called Jorjor Well. so we’re following some random guy’s drama adaptation, and it’s shaping up quite nice if I do say so myself.

ZEPH: Well, that’s great for you guys.

ALEX: still chillin’ in the LNS?

ZEPH: Yeah, there’s no place like it. I really love the atmosphere there.

ALEX: or lack thereof. Heh.

ALEX: it’s boooring.

ZEPH: Psh, that’s what you think.

ALEX: that’s what everyone thinks, pal. why stay in that dusty cramped room with all the projectors and switches and knobs and equalizers when you can be out there, under the spotlight, acting on center stage?

He makes a dramatic clench of his fists, as if on cue, to illustrate his point.

ALEX: the gaze of the audience, transfixed on your every movement. man, knowing you have the power to change the audience, sway your character’s fate, knowing you can be the one essential lens between fiction and reality, doesn’t that excite you?

Zeph assembles every last shred of his melodramatic, performative spirit.

ZEPH: But fiction with all its own bells and whistles is still fiction. Predetermined, calculated, and even if followed to the dot and comma, is still merely an escape from reality which cannot hold forever.

ZEPH: How stressful is it, leading the people watching you into your world of dreams, only to collapse it all into dust when the curtains close for the day?

ZEPH: I simply take no excitement from watching the whimsical or supernatural, and having people pretend it is real.

ZEPH: I like Lights and Sounds precisely because it carries with it its own reality, its own life in the notes and the chords, in the light and in the dark. It is its own pure simulacrum.

ALEX: heh. that was a pretty dramatic monologue, I’ll give you that.

ALEX: maybe you really should be out there acting.

ZEPH: Heheh, big words coming from Mr. Second Lead Role, I guess.

ALEX: ehh, how’d you find that out?

ZEPH: Word gets around. I eavesdrop on people sometimes.

ZEPH: Also come on, you practically radiate deuteragonist energy.

ALEX: aww, I’ll take that as a compliment.

The two talk all the way until the bus leaves their stop, past sunset. By the time they reach their houses, the night has fallen.

ALEX: alright man, great talk today.

ALEX: happy birthday, by the way. don’t think I forgot.

ZEPH: Oh, thanks. I didn’t think too much about it, actually.

ZEPH: My parents are out across the country for work this time of the month, so they did tell me to video call them for today.

ALEX: you’ll be alone for a few days again? why not crash at my place? my parents and I can bring out the inflatable mattress.

ZEPH: My parents would object.

ZEPH: Well, that’s what they would say if they were here!

ALEX: slumber party, woo!!

ZEPH: Heheh, I do want to cook something of my own, though. In case your parents made dinner or something again.

ALEX: oh yeah, my mom texted me saying she was baking a casserole tonight. wait, hang on.

ALEX: you don’t mean your instant ramen stash, do you?

ZEPH: Hell yeah I do.

ALEX: oh my god.

ZEPH: Anyway I’ve already chosen a pretty nice one for today. Tried and tested. It’s a miso flavour.

ALEX: wow. whatever bro, it’s your day. I won’t judge.

ALEX: expect you to be at my door in an hour? I’ll save some cass’ for supper.

ZEPH: Sure, an hour is more than plenty.

ALEX: enjoy your miso, man. see ya 8.20!

ZEPH: See you in a bit, Alex.

You unlock the front door and enter. The house, once filled with warmth, feels cold and almost foreign. Well, that is, until your parents come back in a few days to lighten things up again. You love your parents. Unfortunate that they had to leave the state this morning, of all mornings. Anyway you call them once you settle down. The talk goes great, honestly. It’s happier than approximately 85% of your usual conversations with your parents. Maybe about 50% if you omit all the laundry and dishes reminders. After you and your parents hang up, about 25 minutes have passed, and you’re quite famished.

You open a cabinet meant primarily for laundry detergent, and remove the fresh-smelling fodder to retrieve your prized possessions. Ah who are you kidding, they’re literally just packets of instant ramen. Your parents would murder you if they found out you spend 30% of your school budget by hopping over to the Asian supermart 2 blocks from school every once in a while and popping a few bucks on these babies. You’re no ramen expert, but damn the Asians know their stuff.

You grab a Miso Flavour ramen packet. It’s a well-seasoned taste you discovered a few months ago and have not been able to stop trying ever since. You walk off to grab a scissors to open the packaging, and--

?????: hey.

--wait what?

?????: what do you think you’re doing, buddy?

A calm and composed voice, rather similar to Alex's, echoes throughout the house and yet seems to linger just out of his field of view, trailing behind him like a shadow. A very puzzled Zeph checks the windows to see who it is, and finds absolutely no-body there.

ZEPH: Hold on, Alex. Where are you, exactly?

ZEPH: I don’t think I’ve heard your voice from the outside of the house.

????? giggles softly.

?????: silly boy, i’m not Alex.

?????: i’m inside your head.

?????: don’t talk out loud like that, people will think you’re going insane.

ZEPH: And so I am going insane.

?????: no, you’re not.

?????: you know, i’ve been a part of you since the very beginning.

?????: i’ve been watching, growing up with you, influencing you.

Zeph feels something step through the door and into the kitchen, even though the door is closed.

ZEPH: Oh my god.

ZEPH: What are you?

?????: i’m you.

?????: i’m everything you hate, everything in yourself you want to put away, your worst fears, your nightmares, your failures.

Zeph collapses into a nearby chair as visions swell up in his mind.

?????: why, i’m everything you don’t want to be.

?????: and yet, i’m everything you have, aren’t i?

ZEPH: Holy shit.

ZEPH: That’s not true. You can't say that.

?????: come on, you can’t lie to yourself.

ZEPH: [tearing up] No…I’m not…

?????: you’re surrounded by massively more successful people, who have no time for all your whims and intricacies. you have no one who truly understands you, no one to tell your worries to, no one to be comforted by…

?????: no one to blame but yourself.

ZEPH: Stop this, whoever you are, stop this.

?????: if you ask me, it’s PATHETIC.

ZEPH: STOP IT!

ZEPH: Why are you coming to torment me, to remind me of all this on my birthday? It’s nothing I don’t already know! I’m supposed to be happy, why can’t I feel like it?

?????: hush, sweetie. again, nobody can hear you.

?????: although, that’s exactly my point.

?????: i appear because despite it being your special day, you’re all alone.

Zeph breaks down and sobs quietly.

?????: anyway, cheer up. i haven’t bothered you much today, but i felt like now would be a perfect time to make myself known.

?????: we’re only allowed to reveal ourselves explicitly from a certain age, anyway.

ZEPH: That’s…stupid and weird…

?????: yeah, rules are rules. oh well.

Zeph picks up the scissors to cut open the noodle packaging.

?????: oh, and one last thing. i’ve always wanted to try that really spicy korean one at the back.

ZEPH: …Why should I care about what you want?

?????: woah buddy, i sense some charged language right there.

ZEPH: My first impression of whatever you are isn’t very good.

?????: yeah, i expected that.

?????: anyway, just a suggestion. you don’t have to do what i say.

?????: although i would very much like you to.

?????: which means at least a part of you will enjoy it as well.

ZEPH: …

You pause for a brief moment to contemplate it. You honestly bought that thing as a joke, a cruel prank to play on Alex, or anyone whom you happen to hate one fine day. But you guess the person you hate most right now is yourself.

ZEPH: Fine, whatever. If even my own negativity manifests itself--

?????: excuse me, “herself”.

ZEPH: Hold on, what?

?????: yeah, deal with it. this is the first time i’m using a voice, though, so i just borrowed Alex’s.

ZEPH: That explains it a little bit. Aren’t you supposed to be me?

?????: haven’t you been curious to see who’d you become if you were a girl?

ZEPH: Touche. Don’t tell that to anyone else, though.

?????: don’t worry, i’ll keep it to yourself,

ZEPH: If even my own negativity manifests herself and tells me to try a different flavour of instant ramen on my 17th birthday, then I must really want to try this, eh.

?????: thaaaat’s the spirit!

?????: thank me later.

With that, the pressure and dread that had insidiously seeped into the atmosphere of the kitchen was suddenly lifted. Zeph goes to the ramen stash to examine the spicy ramen packet. It’s touted as “HOT Chicken Flavour Ramen”, meant to be eaten dry. There is an unsettling amount of red in the picture of the noodles on the front. He takes the spicy ramen packet and puts the miso packet back in its stash to be eaten in a few days or so, who knows.

ZEPH: I’ve never been a person who craves spicy foods. Guess I’ll get a verdict tonight…

He cooks the noodles, drains the water and mixes the flavour packets into the bowl. The resulting aroma is extremely enticing, although the noodles look even more red and intimidating than they did as advertised.

ZEPH: Oh boy.

ZEPH: You better be right about this…

He begins eating and the pain gets to him, first on his tongue, and then flaring through his nostrils with subsequent bites. He got up again, two minutes later, to grab a carton of milk from the fridge. It was really damn spicy, but hey, it tasted really good.

A mix of pain and euphoria from the added spice. Zeph was hooked.

A Mid-Autumn Night’s Dream
